Last updated: July 5, 2026

Refill helps you keep track of how many days of a medication you have left. Add a medication with a starting pill count and pills-per-day rate, tap “Took it” each day, and Refill shows your remaining supply as a shrinking blister-pack grid and projects when you’ll need a refill — using an adaptive estimate based on your actual logged usage, not just a fixed rate.

Purchases

Refill Pro is a one-time, non-consumable purchase ($2.99, paid once, not a subscription) that unlocks unlimited medications, full refill history, and custom pill-shape/color themes. The free app fully solves the core problem on its own, forever: track up to 2 medications with daily check-ins, low-supply warnings, notifications, and adaptive refill-date projections.

Frequently asked questions

How do I restore my purchase?
Open the app, go to the Settings tab, and tap “Restore Purchases.” Make sure you are signed in with the same Apple ID you used to purchase.
Is Refill Pro a subscription?
No. It is a single one-time purchase. You pay once and it is yours for good — nothing renews or recurs.
Is my data private?
Yes. Everything you enter stays on your device. Refill has no network access, no account, and no third-party advertising or tracking SDKs. See our Privacy Policy.
Does Refill read my prescription label or count pills automatically?
No. Refill is honest about what it does: any photo you attach is stored purely as your own visual reference. There is no OCR, no text recognition, and no automated pill-counting or machine-learning analysis anywhere in the app. All counts are entered manually by you.
Is Refill a medical or dosage-tracking app?
No. Refill is a supply estimator — it helps you see how many days of medication you likely have left. It does not verify dosage accuracy, diagnose or treat any condition, or replace guidance from your prescriber or pharmacist.
How does the adaptive refill-date projection work?
Refill looks at your actual "took it" check-ins over roughly the last two weeks and computes the pills-per-day you've really been using, blending it with your configured rate as more history accumulates. If you miss days or catch up in a burst, the projected refill date adjusts automatically instead of relying on a fixed rate.
What happens to older refill history on the free tier?
It is never deleted. The free tier just displays the 3 most recent refill events per medication. Everything reappears automatically the moment you unlock Refill Pro.
How do I delete all my data?
Go to Settings → Delete All Data. You'll be asked to confirm, since this permanently removes every medication, check-in, and refill event from your device.
