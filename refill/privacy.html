Refill

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 5, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what Refill (“the app”, “we”) does with your information. Refill is a pill-supply counter, not a medical or dosage-tracking product, and it works entirely on your device: your data never leaves your phone. There is no server, no account, no analytics SDK, and no network call anywhere in this app.

Who we are

The data controller for this app is Shimon Deitel (Joshua Deitel). You can reach us at s0533495227@gmail.com for any privacy question or request.

What data the app handles

  • The medication names, pill counts, pills-per-day rate, and low-supply threshold you enter.
  • The date/time of each “took it” check-in and each refill event you log.
  • An optional photo of your pill bottle or label, kept purely as your own visual reference.
  • Your notification time, default threshold, and haptics preference.
  • Whether you have purchased Refill Pro.

All of this stays on your device, inside the app’s own private storage folder. Nothing is uploaded to us or to any third party, ever.

Photo library permission

Refill requests photo library access solely so you can attach a photo of your pill bottle or label to a medication entry, using Apple’s standard picker. That photo is stored only as a visual reference for you. Refill performs no OCR, no text recognition, no automated pill counting, and no machine-learning analysis of any photo — it never reads label text or verifies prescription information.

Not medical advice

Refill estimates how many days of supply you likely have left based on the counts and check-ins you enter, plus a rolling average of your actual logged usage. It is a supply estimator, not a medical device, dosage tracker, or prescription management tool. It does not verify dosage accuracy, does not diagnose or treat any condition, and should never replace guidance from your prescriber or pharmacist.

Where your data is stored

Your medications, check-ins, refill history, and any reference photos are stored on your device using standard iOS local file storage (your app’s own Application Support directory). If your device is configured to use iCloud device backup, your data may be backed up through your own Apple iCloud account, which is controlled by you and governed by Apple’s privacy policy. We do not operate a server that stores your data, and we cannot access it.

Local notifications

Refill can schedule on-device notifications to warn you when a medication is running low. These notifications are generated and delivered entirely on your device using Apple’s local notification system; no notification content is sent to us or to any third-party push service.

No tracking or advertising

The app does not contain third-party advertising SDKs, and it does not use third-party analytics or tracking frameworks. We do not build advertising profiles from your activity, and we could not share your data with anyone even if we wanted to — the app has no network connectivity of any kind.

Purchases

The app offers an optional one-time, non-consumable purchase (“Refill Pro,” $2.99, purchased once) that unlocks unlimited medications, full refill history, and custom pill-shape/color themes. This is a single purchase, not a subscription — it does not renew or recur, and it is billed once through the App Store. Purchase processing is handled entirely by Apple. We do not receive or store your payment card details, and Pro access is tied to your Apple ID so you can restore it on any device using “Restore Purchases.”

Free-tier users can track up to 2 medications and see the 3 most recent refill events per medication. Older refill events are never deleted — they remain safely stored on-device and become visible again as soon as Pro is purchased or restored.

Children’s privacy

The app is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us at s0533495227@gmail.com and we will address it.

Your rights (GDPR and CCPA/CPRA)

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, or erase your personal data, and the right not to have your personal information sold. We do not sell personal information — we have no way to access it in the first place. Because your content is stored on your device, you can exercise access and erasure directly: open the app to view your medications, use “Delete All Data” in Settings, or delete the app entirely. For any request you cannot complete yourself, email us at s0533495227@gmail.com and we will respond.

Deleting your data

To remove all of the app’s data from your device, use the “Delete All Data” destructive action in Settings (which requires confirmation), or delete the app itself; either removes all locally stored medications, check-ins, refill history, reference photos, and settings. If you sync through your own iCloud device backup, manage or remove that data in your iCloud settings.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update the “Last updated” date above. Continued use of the app after changes means you accept the updated policy.
